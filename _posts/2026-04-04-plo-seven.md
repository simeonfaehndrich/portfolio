## PLO7

> Demonstrate effective collaboration, decision-making, and leadership in team settings

Through my work on the Musqueam Resource Curation Project, I worked at different levels of teams, demonstrating a variety of collaborative skills.

I worked with two other students to schedule a realistic weekly meeting time throughout the term. We then split up research work and the database build work. I took initiative for locating hard to find print material, as well as making the excel spreadsheet updates and documentation we had decided upon. We discussed and made decisions together as to how we would format our diverse research in a consistent way for the project. We developed a strong bond and also met informally to discuss our personal engagement with the project and to support one another with the workload.

We also met weekly with our supervising professor and two staff from CTLT Indigenous Initiatives, and I developed working relationships with them. For example I documented several missing works which I passed along to the CTLT staff responsible, as well as my hypothesis for why they were missed. When the supervising professor was not available to set up the meeting room, I came in early to set up the zoom apparatus (an owl!). I also occasionally brought in baked goods to improve our collaboration.

At the final level, we also met several times over the course of the semester with the archives staff at Musqueam. The first time they were ambivalent about their involvement in the project and later as the long-term significance of this was unclear, I circled back to clarify if they were pulling back from the project in its entirety or merely not looking to expand its scope. Their clearer reiteration was a relief to our team. Later in the term, when one of these archivists visited our Indigenous Initiatives class as a guest lecturer, I made sure to make them feel welcome before class started (our instructor was late). The core team of the project also had to navigate different visions and priorities of stakeholders that I did not meet personally, such as the UBC-Musqueam Liaison, and Campus Community & Planning.
