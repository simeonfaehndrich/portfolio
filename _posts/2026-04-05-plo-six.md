> PLO6 Communicate clearly using a range of media suited to diverse audiences and goals

A project that is near and dear to my heart is A WEEK OF WHAT?! This came out of an individual Research Methodologies assignment where we were tasked to keep track of something as data over the course of a week and visualize the outcome creatively.

I chose to record every instance of miscommunication over one sunny reading break—or more specifically whenever me or someone else said “WHAT?,” “Hunh?,” or “Pardon me.” On theme for communication, I hypothesized making miscommunication explicit might reveal some fascinating trends. I recorded first on my hand, then on my Notes app. I then got to work graphing. I needed the initial vision of the visualization to throw viewers off—to be incomprehensible. This was key for the principle of medium is the message. 

I began with a scribble. This was me. I then made points for the different people I interacted with that week. Then came the interactions. The result was a colourful explosion—just legible with the aid of a minimal legend.

![Image]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/what.jpg)

For this project I wrote a fun little reflection on my methodologies and a discussion of the results, in which I worked on communicating communication and its pitfalls. I noted some conclusions such as how if I was closer with an individual they were both more likely to say “what?” and I was more likely to mumble. There were also limitations with my experimental set up such as my ability to record data points if I was having trouble hearing—especially interactions between two non-me participants. An overall aim of this project was to investigate the fallibility of relentless data collection of all aspects of our lives. My hope was to communicate research in a fun way with a satirical bent to a general audience (my teacher). What?
